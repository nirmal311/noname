The charitible remainder trust is almost a mirror image of the charitable lead trust. After Joe funds the trust, it makes payments back to him or another beneficiary for the life of the trust. This could be the rest of Joe's life or a designated time limit no greater than 20 years. Each year the fund must distribute fixed amount.

If the trust is setup as a charitable remainder unitrust than the fund must pay out a fixed percentage each year. If it is set up as a charitable remainder annuity trust than an actual designated amount of money must be distributed.

When the time is up, the remainder of the money goes to a charity or group of charities.The major benefit of a charitable remainder trust is that expensive, highly appreciating assets such as real estate or stocks can be place in the trust. Since the trust only has to make minimal payments those assets can continue to grow in value. The end result is the charity gets the benefit of those assets' increased worth.
